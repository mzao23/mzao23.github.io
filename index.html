February 21st, 22nd and 23rd

Preamble...

Model United Nations is a simulation program designed to build strong and lasting relationships between the United Nations and participants from around the world. This United Nations program encourages participants to take concrete action in support of UN values and sustainable development goals. To this end, the leaders of the program develop guides and organize workshops - called miniMUNs - that teach young people to make their activities more authentic, by attending UN-style conferences and meetings to share first-hand knowledge of the United Nations.

What is MUN ?

Model United Nations is a United Nations simulation designed to train participants in international negotiations. These simulations aim to promote human rights, communication and international relations skills, as well as the basics of diplomacy. As part of the UN simulations, students take on the role of ambassadors or foreign ministers, participating in simulations of international sections of the UN. Participants are asked to research countries, debates and international issues in an attempt to find solutions to global problems.

The MUN process...

MUNs take place over the space of a few days. Participants gather in one place - often a school - and are divided into several committees, each dealing with different issues and representing a different country or organization, called a "delegation" (e.g. Mexican delegation). The final aim is to sign a resolution at the end of the debates, after presenting their resolutions and/or political texts. The debates are set up to encourage the development of oral argumentation skills, as well as the ability to convince an audience, while respecting the speaking rules of the UN stimulations.

THE PROCEDURE

During the simulations, participants must use various means of communication and negotiations to represent the policies of the countrie they are representing. These simulations therefore involve public speeches as well as the analysis of other international positions, negotiations and conflict resolution. They also involve the drafting of technical papers (working papers: draft resolutions).
